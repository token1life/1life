The whitepaper of 1LIFE Token

Abstract

The 1LIFE token is a meme coin designed to facilitate the collection of donations for its creator. It operates on three networks: MATIC, BNB, and TON.

Introduction

This document introduces the 1LIFE token, outlining its purpose and underlying technology. The 1LIFE token, categorized as a meme coin within the cryptocurrency sphere, has gained popularity for its humorous or entertaining nature. Despite its light-hearted origins, the 1LIFE token serves a serious purpose: enabling the collection of donations for its creator.

The 1LIFE Token

Purpose

The primary aim of the 1LIFE token is to act as a vehicle for collecting donations for its creator. This objective is achieved by harnessing the capabilities of blockchain technology, ensuring secure, transparent, and efficient transactions.

Tokenomics

The 1LIFE token operates on three networks: MATIC, BNB, and TON. This multi-network operation increases the accessibility and versatility of the token, allowing it to reach a wider audience and adapt to various use cases.

The token issuance is 21,000,000 tokens on each network. You can buy the token on decentralized exchanges such as DeDust, 1inch, and Uniswap using limit orders at a price of $1 and above. There will be no additional token issuance.

Technology

Blockchain Networks

The 1LIFE token operates on the BNB, TON and MATIC networks. These networks were chosen for their speed, security, and low transaction fees.

Security

The security of the 1LIFE token is ensured by the inherent properties of the blockchain technology it utilizes. All transactions are transparent and can be traced back to their origin, making fraudulent activity extremely difficult. For the MATIC and BNB networks, I used a copy of the smart contract from examples in the instructions for creating simple smart contracts. I published the source code of the smart contract in the corresponding networks. I am not a professional in smart contract development and I currently have no opportunity to hire auditors. The token in the TON network was created through the infrastructure promoted by the TON network. I recommend buying the token specifically in the TON network. In case the smart contract is hacked in any network, the transaction history is stored in the blockchain and it will always be possible to restore the list of those who bought the token before a potential hack. This may be needed for the distribution of rewards for participation in projects. So you can still use the MATIC and BNB networks if it’s more convenient.
